Optimizing and Predicting Emergency Calls in the SF Bay Area

Much of the data analysis and visualization that I conducted was through Microsoft Excel and Google Sheets. In this website, I will be breaking down the visualizations by question numbers. My calculation for dispatch time was to subtract dispatch_timestamp from entry_timestamp and convert that time into seconds. This allows for an easier value to average for the further questions that involved average dispatch time. My main use of location were zip codes, because it allowed for a direct sorting of multiple values including average dispatch time, dispatch type (unit_type), and call type. However, for problem 3, I looked at the longest average dispatch time for the three location classification systems: zip code, station number, and city. The overall average dispatch time amongst the entire dataset is 225 seconds. I also added Google Maps visualizations of the 3 location categories above to compare and contrast the area that had the longest average dispatch time.


1. Data Visuals: displaying 3 metrics or trends from dataset

1.1: Average dispatch time based on call type

The first trend that I wanted to analyze was the average dispatch time based on the call type. After collectings the data, I decided to have one graph that showed all of the call types and then another that had all of the call types but Trail/Rail Incident. As you can see from the first graph will all of the call types, the average Train/Rail Incident has a significantly longer average dispatch time than any of the other call types at 8298 seconds. The 2nd longest average dispatch time was Water Rescue at 304 seconds. Intuitively, this makes sense because Trains/Rail Incidences are much more difficult to solve and to tend to than single person-based calls like Water Rescues and Elevator/Escalator Rescues.

1.2: Final dispatch distribution

The second metric that I wanted to show was which final dispatch type was the most prevalent within the dataset through analyzing the percent distribution of final dispatch type for all of the calls made in the data set. As can be shown from the donut graph, I have shown the top 4 percentage dispatch types with text. In the graph, Code 2 Transport makes up the majority of the percentage distribution at 50.4% percent of calls. For the second largest, Fire makes up 26.4% of the percentage distribution. The other 10 call types each have AT MOST 5% percent distrubtion. This can be explained because Code 2 is classified as Non-Emergencies, which make up a majority of the country's calls/crimes.

1.3: Call Type Distribution

The third and final trend I wanted to show was the estimated call type based on Final Priority Type. I decided to use a stacked bar chart to not only compare the call type distribution within each Final Priority Type, but also between each Final Priority Type as well. As shown by the graph, for Priority Type 2, the most prevalent call type is Non Life-Threatening, which made up 2183 of the calls out of 2925 in total, which is 74.63% of all Type 2 calls. For Prioirty Type 3, the most prevalent is Potentially Life-Thretening, which made up 4012 of the calls out of 7071 ins total, which is 56.74% of all Type 3 calls. Therefore, depending on the priority type, the expected call type differs.

To find the most likely dispatch to be required, I created 2 graphs; one based on location and another based on time. For the location, I used zip codes. Within that zip code, the most prevalent dispatch type is Engine at 472 of the calls. 3. Areas that take the longest time to dispatch on average

To properly determine the areas that take the longest time to dispatch on average, I plotted the average dispatch times of three area types: zip code, station area, and city. For zip code, there are two zip codes that have a significant average dispatch time longer than the others. The longest being 94127 and the second longest being 94015, each being 1870 seconds and 1586 seconds, respectively. The overall average dispatch time is 225 seconds. For station area, there are two clear frontrunners as well: first being 39 at 1553 seconds and second being 13 at 1158 seconds. Finally, for city, the two longest average dispatch times are first, in Presidio at 291 seconds, and in San Francisco at 225 seconds, which is again the overall average dispatch time. As found on Google Maps, the area code for 94127 covers San Francisco, which is the second largest average dispatch time amongst the cities given in the data set. The area code for 94015 is Daly City, which is a suburb of San Francisco. Finally, Presidio is shown as a neighborhood within San Francisco that is right by the Golden Gate Bridge, which contributes to high traffic due to tourists, which explains the high average dispatch time.
